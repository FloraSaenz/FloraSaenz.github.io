My Computer Science Master's So Far: A Journal Entry

My journey on this degree program has not been easy at all, as you could probably tell from the rest of my webpage I did not start (or develop into) a logic-inclined person at all during my undergrad. However, I WILL say the journey has been much smoother than I had foreseen. If this interests you, keep reading. If not, watch every movie I listed expeditiously.

When I first came into the program I was taking CS1, OOP, and Discrete Math all at once. Now I see just how crazy that was! I knew no coding and I was learning HOW to code and how to implement objects at the same time. As you can imagine, I considered that maybe this path was not for me and that maybe I would never get the hang of it. There were mutliple OOP midterms I walked out of with tears in my eyes during that semester. I got out though! That was more than enough for me to shoot for another semester testing my abilities.

On my second semester, I took Compilers, Intro to Data Structures, and Software Development all at the same time...When I say this semester changed me, I mean it. The Compilers class changed my understanding of programming completely and so did Software Dev. Both of these courses made me prove to myself that I was, in fact, capable of making breakthroughs. The jump on my abilities from the end of my first semester to the end of the second was astronomical. I even felt confident enough to apply to be a TA, and did get a position Taing for OOP...Which is ironic.

This semester, I am sensing that things are looking up for me a great deal in terms of my understanding but I know I still have a great deal to learn and apply. Sometimes I feel like Michael Scott in the sense that sometimes I'll start a sentence (code) and I don't even know where it's going. I just hope I find it along the way.

Cheers to a new semester where I finally learn web design!
